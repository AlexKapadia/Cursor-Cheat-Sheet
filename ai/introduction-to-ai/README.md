## What It Is

This MDC (Markdown Cursor) document is a comprehensive extraction and organization of **Chapter 1: Introduction to AI** from the textbook "Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Deep Learning" by Oswald Campesato (2020, Mercury Learning and Information).

This MDC provides:
- **Complete overview** of artificial intelligence concepts and definitions
- **Historical context** of AI development from early systems to modern approaches
- **Key concepts** including Strong AI vs Weak AI, the Turing Test, heuristics, and more

- **Real-world applications** of AI in games, robotics, healthcare, and more

The MDC is designed to serve as a complete reference for understanding the foundations of AI, providing the conceptual groundwork for subsequent chapters on machine learning and deep learning.

## Key Concepts

This chapter covers numerous key concepts in AI:

1. **Artificial Intelligence:** The science of making machines do things that would require intelligence if done by man
2. **Strong AI vs Weak AI:** Two philosophical approaches to AI development
3. **Turing Test:** Operational test for machine intelligence
4. **Heuristics:** Rules of thumb for problem-solving
5. **Genetic Algorithms:** Evolutionary computation approaches
6. **Knowledge Representation:** Methods for storing and organizing knowledge
7. **Expert Systems:** Rule-based systems encoding domain expertise
8. **Neural Computing:** Computing inspired by biological neural networks
9. **Natural Language Processing:** AI subfield for language understanding
10. **Machine Learning:** Data-driven approach to AI
11. **Deep Learning:** Advanced neural network architectures
12. **Reinforcement Learning:** Trial-and-error learning approach
